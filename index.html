Pick the trip by day.

This is the actual mix-and-match surface: pick one from each row. The full-plan cards below are only suggested combinations.

1. Monday afternoon / evening

After flight + bus + bags. One meaningful cluster is enough.

Easy arrival

Lowest-stress first day. Get fed, sort bags, do one easy nearby church/walk, early dinner.

  • Good if travel hits hard
  • Works before/after 15:00 check-in
  • Best Patrick safety valve

Old Catholic Paris

The most meaningful Monday: Saint‑Nicolas, St‑Étienne, Notre‑Dame exterior, Seine/Latin Quarter dinner.

  • Best Catholic fit
  • Taxi to Latin Quarter
  • Moderate walking

Notre‑Dame + Sainte‑Chapelle

Most iconic central option. Notre‑Dame exterior plus Sainte‑Chapelle if we book a sane time slot.

  • Beautiful stained glass
  • Ticket/security friction
  • Compact area

2. Tuesday morning

This is the best usable morning. Choose the anchor first.

Rue du Bac + Saint‑Germain

Miraculous Medal Chapel, Le Bon Marché / La Grande Épicerie, then Saint‑Germain or Saint‑Sulpice.

  • Strong notes priority
  • Good cafés/shopping
  • Pairs perfectly with west afternoon

Notre‑Dame fallback

Use Tuesday morning for Notre‑Dame/Sainte‑Chapelle if Monday was too tired or ticket timing is better.

  • Protects must-do Notre‑Dame
  • Good if Monday went easy
  • May crowd out shopping

Slow family morning

Pastry/coffee near Airbnb or Saint‑Germain, short walk, keep Patrick steady before the afternoon.

  • Lowest stress
  • Less sightseeing
  • Good if sleep is rough

3. Tuesday afternoon / evening

This is where shopping, Arc, Eiffel-view, or high-friction swaps compete.

Shopping westward

Rue Saint‑Honoré, Place Vendôme, Rue Cambon, Avenue Montaigne, Champs‑Élysées, Arc.

  • Chanel / Goyard / Cartier / Le Labo zone
  • Hermès / luxury flagships west
  • Best match to notes

Classic sights

Tuileries/Louvre exterior, Champs/Arc, then Trocadéro for the Eiffel Tower photo. No Louvre interior Tuesday.

  • Most recognizable Paris
  • Taxi helpful
  • Good photos

Montmartre swap

Sacré‑Cœur and Montmartre if family really wants the view. Pretty, but hilly and stroller-annoying.

  • Optional only
  • High friction
  • Replaces shopping or Arc time
