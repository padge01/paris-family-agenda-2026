Removed: Easy / Balanced / Ambitious as the primary model. They are picking experiences, not intensity.

1. Hero / orientation

One-screen explanation:

  • Paris with Patrick
  • One arrival afternoon + one full Paris day
  • Monday starts after the Beauvais → Porte Maillot airport bus
  • Airbnb check-in is normally 3:00pm
  • Tuesday is the main Paris day
  • Wednesday is airport morning

2. Map-first overview

Map should make the geography intuitive before asking for choices.

  • Porte Maillot / A01 airport bus
  • Apartment at 36 Rue du Faubourg Saint-Martin
  • Old Catholic Paris: Saint-Nicolas, Notre-Dame, Sainte-Chapelle, St-Etienne
  • Rue du Bac / Saint-Germain: Miraculous Medal Chapel, Saint-Sulpice, Le Bon Marche, classic cafés
  • West Paris: shopping streets, Arc, Trocadero / Eiffel view
  • Optional: Sacre-Coeur / Montmartre

7. Food / café / dinner preferences

Choices by experience, not a restaurant database:

  • Easy bakery near apartment: Mamiche / Du Pain et des Idees
  • Classic Saint-Germain café: Café de Flore / Les Deux Magots
  • Elegant pastry / hot chocolate: Angelina
  • Eiffel-view café: Carette if Trocadero route
  • Dinner near apartment
  • Dinner where the day ends

Rule: every food place name must be linked at the point it appears.

8. Seamless in-page submission

Final version must save responses from the page itself.

  • Person enters their name
  • Clicks choices
  • Taps Submit
  • Page confirms response was saved
  • Padraig can review responses in one place
  • No copy-to-chat as final mechanism

Likely implementation: Google Apps Script to Sheet for custom page + simple storage, or Tally/Google Form if low-risk matters more.

Success criteria

Within 20 seconds, a family member should understand:

  • where the airport bus, apartment, Catholic stops, cafés, shopping, and photo stops are
  • that Monday is flexible because check-in is 3:00pm
  • that Tuesday is the main Paris day
  • what they are supposed to click
  • that they are giving preferences, not locking in a rigid itinerary
  • that Submit saves their preferences directly on the site
